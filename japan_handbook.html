Detailed Itinerary

Day 1 (April 16) - Arrival in Tokyo

  • Arrive at Narita Airport
  • Exchange JR Pass voucher
  • Train to Tokyo Station
  • Check-in at hotel in Asakusa area
  • Evening: Explore Sensoji Temple & Nakamise Shopping Street

Day 2 (April 17) - Tokyo Cultural Day

  • Morning: Meiji Shrine & Harajuku
  • Afternoon: Tea Ceremony at Hamarikyu Gardens
  • Evening: Shibuya Crossing & dinner at local izakaya

Day 3 (April 18) - Kamakura Day Trip

  • Morning: Great Buddha (Kotoku-in)
  • Afternoon: Zen Meditation at Engaku-ji Temple
  • Evening: Sunset at Hase-dera Temple (Recommended Proposal Location!)

Day 4 (April 19) - Kyoto Day 1

  • Morning: Shinkansen to Kyoto
  • Afternoon: Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)
  • Evening: Gion District walking tour

Day 5 (April 20) - Kyoto Day 2

  • Morning: Fushimi Inari Shrine
  • Afternoon: Arashiyama Bamboo Grove
  • Evening: Traditional dinner in Pontocho

Day 6 (April 21) - Nara Day Trip

  • Morning: Train to Nara
  • Todai-ji Temple
  • Nara Deer Park
  • Evening: Return to Kyoto

Day 7 (April 22) - Return to Tokyo

  • Morning: Shinkansen to Tokyo
  • Afternoon: Akihabara or Ueno Park
  • Evening: Farewell dinner at Tokyo Skytree

Proposal Location Recommendation

Recommended Location: Hase-dera Temple, Kamakura

Why it's perfect:

  • Beautiful ocean views from the observation deck
  • Famous for its stunning gardens and hydrangeas
  • Less crowded than major Tokyo spots
  • Romantic sunset viewing spot
  • Traditional Japanese temple atmosphere

Best time: 30 minutes before sunset at the observation deck

Travel Tips

  • Transportation:
    • Get a 7-day JR Pass before leaving the US
    • Download offline maps and train schedules
    • Get a IC card (Pasmo/Suica) for local transit
  • Money:
    • Japan is still largely cash-based
    • Get cash at 7-11 ATMs (most reliable for foreign cards)
    • Inform your bank about travel plans
  • Cultural Tips:
    • Remove shoes when entering temples and traditional restaurants
    • Bow slightly when greeting people
    • No tipping is required
    • Be quiet on trains and buses
  • Packing:
    • Comfortable walking shoes
    • Portable battery pack
    • Light jacket for evenings
    • Pocket WiFi (reserve before arrival)
